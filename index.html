RESEARCH INTEREST

Our work spans synthesis, characterization and device physics of advanced materials.

Color-coded categories

Perovskites & Optoelectronics

Low‑defect halide perovskites for solar cells, LEDs, and photodetectors; stability engineering and ion migration.

Optoelectronics

High‑Entropy Alloys & Oxides

Configurational entropy effects, sluggish diffusion, catalytic activity for OER/HER, and structural disorder.

HEAs

Energy Storage Materials

Solid‑state electrolytes, Na‑ion and Li‑ion cathodes, interface physics, and dendrite suppression.

Batteries

2D Materials & Heterostructures

MoS₂, graphene, MXenes for sensing and catalysis; defect engineering and strain‑tunable properties.

Research Objectives

What we aim to achieve in the next 3–5 years.

Roadmap
1

Device‑grade stability in perovskite photovoltaics

Realize >2,000 h operational stability via defect passivation, compositional engineering, and encapsulation.

2

Entropy‑engineered catalysts

Leverage high‑entropy design to minimize overpotentials in HER/OER with Earth‑abundant elements.

3

Safe solid‑state batteries

Design ionically conductive, mechanically robust electrolytes and stable interfaces for high‑rate cycling.

4

Open science & training

Publish datasets, open‑source analysis code, and deliver hands‑on workshops for students and partners.
